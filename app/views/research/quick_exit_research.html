Hide this page

What are we proposing

We are looking to implement a pattern, using the existing button component, that allows vulnerable users of a government service or website to quickly and safely hide the page and avoid putting themselves at risk.

The functionality behind the design pattern would allow a user to quickly hide the current page and open a new, ambiguous webpage, such as BBC Weather or another such website. This would lower the risk of drawing attention to browsing habits from a perpetrator.

User Story

{{ govukInsetText({ classes: 'user-story', text: "As someone who is at risk, I need to quickly and safely be able to change or close my browser window and clear my browser history to prevent someone else from seeing my screen and search history." }) }}

We are working with the Department for Work and Pensions, the Cabinet Office and the Scottish Government to improve and unify this component.

More information comments can be viewed on the Design System backlog issue #213

What has been done so far

The Check Legal Aid team have created an MVP design on specific areas of the service where vulnerable users may need to exit or hide the webpage. This work has been tested with a subject matter expert (SME) who has provided various levels of feedback on the work so far.

MVP design work

We have iterated twice on this design component on our live service, looking at language and functionality.

1. Design

This is our second iteration of the design. We looked to extend the GOV.UK Button component to act as the interactive element that a user would press to exit the website.

The Design System suggests using red for any destructive actions undertaken on a GOV.UK website - as the intention for the 'Hide this page' button was essentially closing the webpage down, it would be deemed destructive.

We have experimented with alternative designs around use of colour, however decided to keep the red button. This element on the design has been taken forward by DWP and potentially the Scottish Government.

We decided to utilise the additional space available in the 1/3 column, which is usually reserved for additional information as it allowed us to present the button within immediate view for the user. The button would then be "sticky" in position at the bottom of the page on smaller devices.

Challenges

  • Iterate on the button overlaying content that appears in the side navigation column
  • Continue work to unify the exact size of the button
  • Explore positioning the button at the top of the user's device on mobile

2. Content / Language

The language used in the button has recently been changed in order to create a more unified style as we continue to work with DWP, Cabinet Office and the Scottish Government. The use of 'Hide this page' presented more of a sense of immediacy, as well as being more descriptive of what the button actually does when the user interacts with the button, so we have moved away from 'Quick Exit'.

We also use a keyboard function for the user to be able to interact with the button which is controlled by pressing the 'Esc' key.

Challenges

  • Finalise language of component
  • Look at whether we need to include esc key content

3. Behaviour / Functionality

We have explored several types of behaviour for the button, however in order to provide the greatest amount of safety for users we decided to implement functionality similar to other charities and organisations.

When a user interacts with the button, they are sent to a new browser tab that opens BBC Weather. The original tab that they were on doesn't close, but redirects to Google.

Challenges

  • Make sure the functionality works without Javascript
  • Explore making the browser on mobile devices open a new tab

Summary highlights from SME

This testing and feedback has been captured in a Miro board

Hightlights from the Miro board are captured below.

What worked well

  • Nice and clear button
  • Button isn't intrusive as you can just scroll down without pressing it
  • The button is better than other organisations that use a similar thing
  • Wording is concise and doesn't take long to scan
  • Use of the red colour is perfect to use as a quick escape

What didn't work well

  • On a mobile the button didn't open the site in a new tab.
  • On a desktop the button hides some of the side information.
Victims of Domestic Abuse are used to using their reptilian brain and are in fight or flight mode so the red would be a trigger for them to draw their to attention to it.
- Comment from SME

Data analytics

The 'Hide this page' button has been running as an MVP on the Check Legal Aid service since 14 April 2020. It has been used fairly often, however at present we are are yet to analyse the data in more data.

Assumptions based on data

  • The 'Esc' button is rarely used - do we still need it?
  • Mobile users use the button more than desktop - do we need to focus on mobile first functionality?
  • Are mobile users accidentally pressing the button due to positioning?
  • Use of the button is spread relatively evenly across the country - how can we analyse that information?

Next steps

We are continuing to work with DWP, Cabinet Office but also the Scottish Government who have used this functionality on their new website for quite some time.

The button has been reviewed by Digital Accessibility Centre specialists, and we will be making improvements to improve the user experience for assistive technologies.

In addition to this, there is an Online Safety Working Group, which is made up of representatives in DWP, GDS, Cabinet Office and MoJ who meet monthly to discuss how we can ensure users of Government services online can be kept safe.
